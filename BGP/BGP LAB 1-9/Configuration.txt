Special Characters
+++++++++++++++++++++++++++

 ^ 	- It searches at the beginning of the line  (Check from Left) (AS-Path start from Right)
 $ 	- It searches at the end of the line 
 _	- Has multiple characteristics. It means a  "^"  oR  "$"  oR  "  "  (Wild Card)

 .*	- Any

  ip as-path access-list [1-500] [Deny/Permit] Expression

Lab # 7 - Configuring Route Aggregation – (AS-Set option) Notes
*******************************************************************
-If you didn't specify any additional options to the command, it will create a new prefix in the BGP table, with an (empty AS_PATH).
-The new prefix will automatically have the weight value of 32768 and get a special attribute called (ATOMIC_AGGREGATE assigned).
-The (ATOMIC AGGREGATE) attribute is informational, and tells the other BGP speakers that this prefix is a result of route aggregation 
-and some information (like AS_PATH , NEXT_HOP or other attributes) from the original prefixes may be missing.
-BGP attaches another attribute called (AGGREGATOR) to the summarized prefix. 
-This attribute specifies the AS number and the BGP router-ID of the aggregating router. Just like the ATOMIC AGGREGATE, this is informational Attribute
***************************************************************************************************************
AS-SET option:
==================
-Aggregation hides information previously found in the specific prefixes. This includes all attributes, such as  AS_PATH , NEXT_HOP and so on.
-The new prefix appears to be originated from within the local AS.
-This causes no problems if all specific prefixes belong to the local AS.
-However, when you summarize prefixes learned from other AS , (information hiding may result in the following)
-Suboptimal routing, due to the loss of path information, such as AS_PATH, MED and so on.
-Routing loops, as removal of AS_PATH attribute and replacement it with an empty list will prevent BGP loop-detection mechanism from working properly.
-To overcome it, it is possible to insert a special new member into the AS_PATH of the newly created summary prefix.
-This can be done with an option called AS_SET
-When BGP receives a prefix it scans the AS_PATH attribute. 
-If the local AS number is found in any of the AS_SET or AS_SEQUENCE elements, the prefix is dropped. (loop prevention mechanism.)
-By default, the aggregated address in BGP will not include the AS-Set information.
-In order to force the use of this information, specify the as-set option
